# Gap Analysis

Gap analysis helps managers and employees identify where skill levels fall short of expectations. By comparing individual scores against team or role averages, you can prioritize development efforts effectively.

## What Is a Gap?

A **gap** is the difference between a team member's score and a reference benchmark. Gaps can be positive (above average) or negative (below average).

- **Negative gap** -- The member scores below the benchmark. This is a development opportunity.
- **Zero gap** -- The member meets the benchmark exactly.
- **Positive gap** -- The member exceeds the benchmark. This is a strength to leverage.

## Reference Benchmarks

The gap analysis supports two benchmarks:

### Team Average

The mean score for each skill across all evaluated team members in the selected assessment. This shows how an individual compares to the rest of the team.

### Role Average

The mean score for each skill across all team members who share the same role. This is more targeted and accounts for different expectations across roles (e.g., a senior developer vs. a junior developer).

::: tip
Use team average for broad comparisons and role average for role-specific performance discussions.
:::

## Reading the Gap View

The gap analysis is presented as a table or bar chart, depending on the view:

| Column | Description |
|---|---|
| Skill | The individual skill being measured |
| Current Score | The team member's score for this skill |
| Benchmark | The team or role average for this skill |
| Gap | The difference (Current Score minus Benchmark) |

Negative gaps are typically highlighted in red or orange to draw attention, while positive gaps may be shown in green.

## Accessing Gap Analysis

1. Navigate to a team member's evaluation detail page
2. Select the **Gap Analysis** tab or section
3. Choose your benchmark (team average or role average) from the toggle or dropdown

The gap data updates when you change the assessment selector or the benchmark type.

## Practical Applications

- **Development plans** -- Focus training budgets on skills with the largest negative gaps.
- **Hiring decisions** -- Identify which skills the team collectively lacks and hire accordingly.
- **Promotion readiness** -- Check whether an employee meets or exceeds the role average for the next level.
- **Self-evaluation comparison** -- Combine gap analysis with self-evaluations to uncover blind spots where self-perception differs from manager assessment.

::: warning
Gap analysis is most meaningful when you have evaluations for at least three or four team members. With fewer data points, averages can be misleading.
:::
